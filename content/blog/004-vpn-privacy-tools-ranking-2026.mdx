# The Best VPNs for Crypto Privacy in 2026 — Ranked and Tested

Not all VPNs are created equal. Most "privacy" VPNs log your data, sell it to advertisers, or crumble under legal pressure. If you're using a VPN to protect crypto transactions, a privacy failure isn't an inconvenience — it's a financial risk.

We tested 14 popular VPNs across six categories that matter to crypto holders. Only 4 made the cut.

## What We Tested

Each VPN was evaluated on:

1. **Logging policy** — Do they actually log? Have they been audited? Have they been tested in court?
2. **Payment options** — Can you pay with crypto? Cash?
3. **Speed & reliability** — Connection speeds, uptime, and consistency across 30 days
4. **Kill switch** — Does it actually work when the connection drops?
5. **Jurisdiction** — Is the company based in a Five Eyes / Fourteen Eyes country?
6. **Open source** — Can the community audit the code?

## The Results

- **Payment:** Cash by mail, Bitcoin, Monero. No email required to sign up.
- **Speed:** 850+ Mbps average on WireGuard. Consistently fast.
- **Kill switch:** Rock solid. Tested across 50+ disconnection scenarios.
- **Jurisdiction:** Sweden (EU, but strong privacy laws)

Mullvad is the gold standard. Account numbers instead of usernames. No email. Send cash in an envelope if you want. It's privacy as a principle, not a marketing angle.

### Tier 3: Failed Our Tests

The following VPNs failed one or more critical criteria:

- **NordVPN** — Has been breached (2018 server compromise). Logging claims are not independently verifiable. Based in Panama but owned by Lithuanian parent company.
- **ExpressVPN** — Acquired by Kape Technologies (formerly Crossrider, an adware company). Trust is compromised.
- **Surfshark** — Merged with NordVPN under same parent. Shared infrastructure concerns.
- **CyberGhost** — Also owned by Kape Technologies. Failed independent logging audit.
- **Private Internet Access** — Also acquired by Kape Technologies. Three strikes.
- **IPVanish** — Caught providing logs to the FBI in 2016 despite "no-logs" claims.

## VPN Best Practices for Crypto

- **Always enable the kill switch** — A VPN that drops connection without killing traffic exposes your real IP
- **Use WireGuard protocol** — Faster and more secure than OpenVPN for most use cases
- **Pay with crypto** — Don't link your VPN to your identity via credit card
- **Use multi-hop for high-value transactions** — Routes through 2+ servers for additional protection
- **Don't rely solely on a VPN** — Combine with Tor for maximum anonymity on sensitive operations
- **Test for DNS leaks** — Use dnsleaktest.com after connecting to verify no leaks

## The Setup We Recommend

For most crypto holders:

- **Daily use:** Mullvad or ProtonVPN on all devices
- **High-value transactions:** Mullvad multi-hop + Tor Browser

Your ISP can see every exchange you visit, every DeFi protocol you interact with, and every wallet address you look up. A VPN is the minimum viable privacy tool.

But the wrong VPN is worse than no VPN — it gives you a false sense of security while logging everything. Stick with audited, no-log providers that accept crypto and have been tested under pressure.
