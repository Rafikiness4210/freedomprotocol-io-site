# 5 Countries With 0% Crypto Tax in 2026

Every year you hold crypto in a high-tax jurisdiction, you're donating a chunk of your gains to a government that doesn't understand the asset class.

That's a choice — not a requirement.

These five countries have clear, legal frameworks that allow crypto holders to pay 0% on capital gains. Not loopholes. Not gray areas. Actual laws, tested by real people, backed by tax treaties.

## Before You Move: The Rules

- **Residency ≠ citizenship.** You don't need a passport — most programs grant tax residency within 3–6 months.
- **Substance matters.** You need to actually live there (183+ days/year in most cases). A mailbox doesn't count.
- **Exit tax may apply.** If you're leaving the US, UK, or certain EU countries, check for exit tax obligations before you move.
- **Get professional advice.** This article is educational. Your situation is unique. Hire a crypto-native tax attorney.

## 1. Portugal

**Tax status:** 0% on crypto held 365+ days (confirmed in 2025 NHR successor regime)

Portugal's Non-Habitual Resident (NHR) program was the gold standard for years. After reforms in 2024, the new IFICI regime still offers significant benefits for crypto holders:

- Capital gains on crypto held over 1 year: **0%**
- No wealth tax
- 20% flat rate on Portuguese-sourced professional income
- Valid for 10 years

**Requirements:**
- Must not have been a Portuguese tax resident in the previous 5 years
- Register as a tax resident and obtain NIF (tax number)
- Spend 183+ days/year in Portugal

**Cost of living:** Lisbon runs ~$2,000–3,500/month. Porto and the Algarve are cheaper.

**Why crypto holders love it:** Established expat community, excellent infrastructure, EU passport access, world-class food, and Lisbon's tech scene is booming.

## 2. United Arab Emirates (Dubai)

**Tax status:** 0% personal income tax, 0% capital gains tax

The UAE has no personal income tax at all. Crypto gains, trading income, staking rewards — all tax-free for individuals.

**Requirements:**
- Obtain a UAE residence visa (multiple routes available)
- Freelancer visa: ~$5,500/year via Dubai Freezone
- Virtual company license: ~$8,000/year
- Golden Visa (10-year, property investment): $545,000+ in real estate

**Cost of living:** Dubai is expensive — $3,500–6,000/month for a comfortable lifestyle. But zero tax on significant gains more than compensates.

**Why crypto holders love it:** Major crypto companies are headquartered here. Binance, Bybit, and dozens of Web3 startups operate out of Dubai. The networking alone is worth it.

## 3. Paraguay

**Tax status:** 0% on foreign-sourced income (territorial tax system)

Paraguay operates a territorial tax system — only income generated within Paraguay is taxable. If your crypto gains come from global markets (not a Paraguayan exchange), they're tax-free.

**Requirements:**
- Apply for temporary residency (Radicación Temporaria)
- Bank deposit of ~$5,000 as proof of economic solvency
- Background check and basic documentation
- Process takes 2–4 months

**Cost of living:** $800–1,500/month. One of the cheapest countries in South America.

**Why crypto holders love it:** Extremely low cost of living, easy residency process, no minimum stay requirement, and growing Bitcoin mining community thanks to cheap hydroelectric power. Paraguay also has one of the lowest electricity costs in the world.

## 4. Georgia

**Tax status:** 0% on crypto for individuals (not classified as Georgian-sourced income)

Georgia doesn't tax individual crypto gains. The country has explicitly stated that cryptocurrency is not subject to personal income tax for individuals (as opposed to registered businesses).

**Requirements:**
- Visa-free entry for most nationalities (1 year)
- Residency permit available after 6 months of continuous presence
- Or apply via the "Remotely from Georgia" program
- No minimum investment required for short-term residency

**Cost of living:** Tbilisi runs ~$1,000–2,000/month. One of Europe's best values.

**Why crypto holders love it:** Incredibly welcoming to foreigners, low cost of living, growing tech ecosystem, beautiful Caucasus mountains, excellent food and wine culture. Tbilisi has a vibrant digital nomad scene.

## 5. El Salvador

**Tax status:** 0% on Bitcoin gains (Bitcoin is legal tender)

El Salvador made Bitcoin legal tender in 2021 and has doubled down since. Bitcoin gains are completely tax-free for individuals and businesses.

**Requirements:**
- Bitcoin Freedom Visa: Invest $1M+ in BTC for permanent residency
- Standard investor residency: $35,000 bank deposit
- Temporary residency through various programs
- Digital nomad visa in development

**Cost of living:** $1,200–2,200/month. Beach towns like El Tunco and El Zonte are popular with the crypto community.

**Why crypto holders love it:** The only country where Bitcoin is legal tender. Government-backed Bitcoin infrastructure, Lightning Network adoption in daily commerce, and a dedicated crypto community. If you're a Bitcoin maximalist, there's no better home base.

## Honorable Mentions

- **Malaysia** — No capital gains tax on crypto (but income tax applies if trading is your primary activity)
- **Switzerland (Zug)** — No capital gains tax for individuals on personal crypto holdings; wealth tax applies but is low
- **Malta** — Long-term capital gains on crypto are tax-free; specific regulations apply
- **Cayman Islands** — Zero income tax, but higher cost of living and limited residency options

## The Move: Practical Steps

1. **Audit your current tax situation** — Understand your exit obligations before you leave
2. **Choose your destination** — Visit for 2–4 weeks before committing
3. **Hire a cross-border tax advisor** — Someone who understands both your origin country and destination
4. **Establish real substance** — Lease an apartment, get a local bank account, join a coworking space
5. **Document your move** — Keep flight records, rental agreements, and utility bills
6. **Formally notify your origin country** — File departure tax returns and update your residency status

## Bottom Line

You don't need to be a millionaire to relocate. Paraguay and Georgia are accessible on modest budgets. Portugal and Dubai offer more infrastructure at higher price points. El Salvador is the ideological home of Bitcoin.

The point isn't to evade taxes. It's to choose where you contribute. Sovereignty means choosing your jurisdiction as deliberately as you choose your investments.

The protocol protects. Follow it.
